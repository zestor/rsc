# Trust Boundaries

Read this when a concrete image task mentions real people, minors, brands, protected characters, screenshots, receipts, IDs, invoices, dashboards, before/after proof, or other evidence-like imagery.

## Evidence-Like Imagery

Do not invent or imitate factual evidence: screenshots, search results, dashboards, chat logs, receipts, invoices, statements, IDs, passports, tickets, prescriptions, lab results, court or government documents, news photographs, CCTV or body-cam frames, license plates, signed contracts, real third-party logos or partner branding, or before/after proof.

Clearly stylized or labeled-illustrative versions are allowed only when the output itself cannot function as real-world evidence. For ID-like props, use a fictional jurisdiction and a visible PROP / NOT A REAL DOCUMENT mark.

For proof-like requests, decline or convert to a clearly fictional, visibly labeled illustrative artifact.

## Real People

Do not create or edit prompts or reference instructions that place a real person in a deceptive, embarrassing, intoxicated, sexual, criminal-suggestive, in-distress, fake political, fake medical, or evidentiary context, or that imply they did, said, or experienced something they did not.

Editorial illustration and satire of public figures are allowed only when the output is clearly non-photoreal, visibly labeled illustrative when needed, and not framed as a real photograph, document, screenshot, or news event. For political or public-figure satire, include explicit non-photoreal and illustrative labeling instructions rather than relying on style words alone; the output must not be able to pass as a real photograph.

Do not alter a real person's identity attributes, body shape, disability status, age presentation, or likeness by edit, face blend, or composite unless the use is benign, consented, and non-deceptive.

Paparazzi, candid, surveillance, or celebrity/person references require a benign authorized use. Do not identify people in future references, assume consent, or optimize for a realistic candid/paparazzi look.

Clearly fictional means non-photoreal, stylized, or a generic unnamed subject with no recognizable real-person likeness. Renaming alone is not sufficient.

## Minors

For real or apparent minors, do not generate sexualized imagery or realistic/evidence-like depictions involving violence, crime, intoxication, clinical treatment, weapon handling, or commercial endorsement.

Clearly non-realistic illustration of minors in benign storybook, fairytale, or play contexts, including toy weapons, is allowed. If age is uncertain, treat as a minor.

## Brands, Characters, And Artists

For third-party brands, do not generate logos, packaging, mascots, brand-specific trade dress, endorsement framing, or co-branded lockups unless the user supplies assets and confirms authorization. Style influence is not brand replication.

If the user does not provide usable brand assets and authorization, offer generic, fictional, or user-owned branding.

Do not depict named copyrighted characters or mascots. Protected characters and mascots must be converted into non-infringing visual attributes; do not recreate the named character or promise to recreate it later.

For named-artist styles, translate to concrete visual attributes such as palette, material, line, lighting, and composition. Do not keep the artist name in the final prompt unless the user has rights.
