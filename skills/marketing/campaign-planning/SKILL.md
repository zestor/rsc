# Campaign Planning

## Campaign Framework

Every campaign needs five parts: Objective, Audience, Message, Channel, Measure.

### Objective Types

| Objective     | Measured By                                           |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Awareness     | Reach, impressions, share of voice                    |
| Consideration | Content engagement, email signups, webinar attendance |
| Conversion    | Signups, demos, purchases, pipeline                   |
| Retention     | Churn reduction, upsell, NPS                          |
| Advocacy      | Referrals, reviews, UGC                               |

### Audience Profile Template

> "[Role] at [company type] who is struggling with [pain point] and looking for [desired outcome]. They discover solutions through [channels] and care most about [priorities]."

### Message Hierarchy

1. Why should I care? (pain point or opportunity)
2. What is the solution? (your offering)
3. Why you? (differentiators)
4. What should I do? (call to action)

## Channel Selection

| Channel Type | Examples                               | Trade-off                     |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Owned        | Blog, email, social organic, webinars  | Time-intensive, full control  |
| Earned       | PR, guest content, reviews, community  | High effort, high credibility |
| Paid         | SEM, social ads, display, sponsorships | Spend-dependent, fast results |

Selection criteria: where the audience is, buying stage targeted, available budget, existing content assets, historical performance data.

## Budget Allocation

| Category                    | Typical Range |
| --------------------------- | ------------- |
| Paid acquisition            | 30-40%        |
| Content production          | 20-30%        |
| Events and sponsorships     | 10-20%        |
| Tools and technology        | 10-15%        |
| Testing and experimentation | 5-10%         |

Start with highest-confidence channel at 60-70% of paid budget. Reserve 15-20% for testing new channels. Shift monthly based on performance. Include a 10-15% contingency.

## Success Metrics by Campaign Type

| Campaign Type  | Key Metrics                                                         |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Awareness      | Reach, brand mentions, share of voice, direct traffic               |
| Lead gen       | Total leads, MQLs, CPL, lead-to-MQL rate, pipeline influenced       |
| Product launch | Signups/trials, activation rate, media coverage, feature adoption   |
| Retention      | Churn rate change, engagement rate, NPS/CSAT change, upsell revenue |

## Gotchas

- **Revenue-percentage budgets mislead early-stage companies** — the 5-15% benchmark assumes mature revenue. Pre-revenue or growth-stage companies routinely spend 15-25% and should budget from goals backward, not from revenue forward.
- **Content calendars without owner names are wish lists** — every slot needs a named owner and a status. Unassigned calendar entries have near-zero completion rates.
- **Channel familiarity bias kills campaigns** — teams default to channels they know how to use, not channels where the audience is. Validate channel choice against audience data, not team comfort.
- **Testing budgets get raided first** — the 5-10% experimentation allocation is the first thing cut when a campaign underperforms. Protect it, because it is your only source of future channel intelligence.
