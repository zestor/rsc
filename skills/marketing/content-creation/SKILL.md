# Content Creation

## Channel-Specific Writing Guidance

### Blog

- Write at 8th-grade reading level for broad audiences; adjust up for technical
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences), subheadings every 200-300 words
- Include at least one data point, example, or quote per section
- Front-load key information in each section

### Social Media

- **LinkedIn**: professional but human, 1,300 characters is the sweet spot before "see more", personal stories and lessons perform well
- **Twitter/X**: strong opening words, threads for longer narratives, engage with replies
- **Instagram**: visual-first, storytelling hooks, line breaks for readability, hashtags in first comment
- **Facebook**: conversational, questions drive comments, under 80 characters gets more engagement for link posts

### Email

- Subject lines under 50 characters — urgency, curiosity, or clear value
- Preview text complements the subject line, does not repeat it
- One primary CTA per email, visually distinct
- Mobile-first: most email is read on mobile

### Landing Pages

- Lead with benefits, not features
- Use "you" language
- Every section answers "so what?" from the reader's perspective
- Reduce friction: fewer form fields, trust signals near CTAs

## SEO Fundamentals

- One primary keyword and 2-3 secondary keywords per piece
- Primary keyword in: headline, first paragraph, one subheading, meta description, URL slug
- Title tag under 60 characters, meta description under 160 characters
- Structure for featured snippets: definition paragraphs, numbered lists, tables
- Internal links (2-3 to related content) and external links (1-2 to authoritative sources)

## Headline Formulas

| Formula                               | Example                                                |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| How to [result] [without obstacle]    | "How to Double Open Rates Without Sending More Emails" |
| [Number] [adjective] ways to [result] | "7 Proven Ways to Reduce Churn"                        |
| Why [common belief] is wrong          | "Why More Content Is Not the Answer"                   |
| [Do this], not [that]                 | "Build a Community, Not Just an Audience"              |
| What [result] taught us about [topic] | "What 10,000 A/B Tests Taught Us About Subject Lines"  |

## CTA Best Practices

- Action verbs: "Get", "Start", "Download", "Join", "Try"
- Specific about what happens next: "Start your free trial" over "Submit"
- Reduce risk: "No credit card required", "Cancel anytime"
- One primary CTA per page or email
- Place above the fold on landing pages, after establishing value in emails, at the end of blog posts

## Gotchas

- **SEO keyword density is not a real metric** — there is no target percentage. If copy reads unnaturally, you have over-optimized. Write for humans; include keywords where they fit naturally.
- **"See more" truncation kills LinkedIn posts** — if the hook before the fold is weak, engagement drops to near zero. The first 2-3 lines must carry the entire post's weight.
- **Blog posts without distribution are invisible** — publishing is not a distribution strategy. Every post needs a promotion plan (email, social, paid amplification, syndication).
- **Subject line A/B tests need 1,000+ recipients per variant** — testing with small lists produces noise, not signal. Below that threshold, just pick the better subject line and move on.
- **Landing pages with navigation links leak conversions** — every outbound link is an exit ramp. Dedicated landing pages should strip site navigation and keep only the CTA.
