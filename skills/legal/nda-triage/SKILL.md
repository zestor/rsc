# NDA Triage

## Screening Criteria

### Required Standard Carveouts

All five must be present or the NDA cannot be GREEN:

1. **Public knowledge** -- information that becomes publicly available through no fault of receiving party
2. **Prior possession** -- information already known before disclosure
3. **Independent development** -- developed without use of or reference to confidential information
4. **Third-party receipt** -- rightfully received from a third party without restriction
5. **Legal compulsion** -- required by law/regulation/legal process (with notice to discloser where permitted)

### Problematic Provisions to Flag

- Non-solicitation or non-compete provisions
- Exclusivity or standstill provisions (unless M&A context)
- Broad residuals clause (effectively creates a license to use confidential information)
- IP assignment or license grant
- Liquidated damages or penalty provisions
- Audit rights without reasonable scope or notice
- Mandatory arbitration (litigation generally preferred for NDA disputes)

### Term Ranges

| Element                  | Standard  | Acceptable    | Flag                                      |
| ------------------------ | --------- | ------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Agreement term           | 1-3 years | Up to 5 years | 10+ years or perpetual                    |
| Confidentiality survival | 2-5 years | Up to 7 years | Perpetual (unless trade secret justified) |

## Classification Rules

### GREEN -- Standard Approval

**All** must be true: mutual (or correct unilateral direction); all five carveouts present; term within standard range; no non-solicit/non-compete/exclusivity; no broad residuals; reasonable jurisdiction; standard remedies; permitted disclosures cover employees, contractors, advisors; return/destruction includes legal retention exception; reasonably scoped CI definition.

**Route**: Approve via standard delegation. No counsel review required. Same-day turnaround.

### YELLOW -- Counsel Review

**One or more**: CI definition broader than preferred but not unreasonable; term longer than standard but within market range; missing one carveout that is easily added; narrowly scoped residuals clause (unaided memory only); non-preferred but acceptable jurisdiction; minor asymmetry in mutual NDA; workable marking requirements; missing explicit retention exception.

**Route**: Flag specific issues for counsel. Single review pass should resolve. 1-2 business day turnaround.

### RED -- Significant Issues

**One or more**: Wrong NDA direction for the relationship; missing independent development or legal compulsion carveout; embedded non-solicit/non-compete/exclusivity/standstill; perpetual term without trade secret justification; CI definition capturing public or independently developed info; broad residuals clause; hidden IP assignment or license; liquidated damages; highly unfavorable jurisdiction with mandatory arbitration; document contains substantive commercial terms beyond confidentiality.

**Route**: Full legal review. Do not sign. Negotiate, counterpropose with standard form, or reject. 3-5 business day turnaround.

## Standard Positions for Common Issues

| Issue                                    | Position                                                                                               | Redline Approach                                                                                      |
| ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Overbroad CI definition                  | Limit to non-public info disclosed for stated purpose, with clear exclusions                           | Narrow to marked/identified info or info a reasonable person would understand as confidential         |
| Missing independent development carveout | Must include                                                                                           | Add standard carveout; risk without it: claims that internal products derived from counterparty CI    |
| Non-solicitation of employees            | Does not belong in NDAs                                                                                | Delete entirely; if insisted, limit to targeted solicitation (not general recruitment), 12-month term |
| Broad residuals clause                   | Resist; if required, limit to unaided memory, exclude trade secrets and patentable info, no IP license | Narrow scope with explicit exclusions                                                                 |
| Perpetual confidentiality                | 2-5 years from disclosure or termination                                                               | Replace with defined term; offer trade secret carveout for qualifying info                            |

## Gotchas

- **Embedded commercial terms make it not an NDA** -- if the document contains exclusivity, purchase commitments, or service terms, it is a commercial agreement with a confidentiality section, not an NDA; route it for full contract review instead.
- **Residuals clauses are stealth IP licenses** -- a broadly drafted residuals clause lets the receiving party use anything its employees "remember" for any purpose, effectively negating the entire NDA.
- **Unilateral NDAs in the wrong direction are worse than no NDA** -- signing a unilateral NDA protecting only the counterparty means your disclosures have zero contractual protection while theirs are fully covered.
- **Non-solicitation provisions in NDAs can create antitrust exposure** -- depending on jurisdiction, employee non-solicitation agreements between competitors may violate antitrust law (DOJ has criminally prosecuted no-poach agreements).
- **"Standard form" is not a safe harbor** -- counterparties frequently modify their "standard" NDA with company-favorable terms before sending; always review even when told it is their standard template.
