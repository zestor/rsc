# Risk Assessment

## Severity x Likelihood Matrix

**Risk Score = Severity x Likelihood**

| Score Range | Level         | Color  |
| ----------- | ------------- | ------ |
| 1-4         | Low Risk      | GREEN  |
| 5-9         | Medium Risk   | YELLOW |
| 10-15       | High Risk     | ORANGE |
| 16-25       | Critical Risk | RED    |

### Severity Scale

| Level | Label      | Description                                                                                                                      |
| ----- | ---------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Negligible | No material financial, operational, or reputational impact                                                                       |
| 2     | Low        | Minor financial exposure (< 1% of contract/deal value); no public attention                                                      |
| 3     | Moderate   | Material financial exposure (1-5%); potential limited public attention                                                           |
| 4     | High       | Substantial exposure (5-25%); likely public attention; potential regulatory scrutiny                                             |
| 5     | Critical   | Major exposure (> 25%); fundamental business disruption; regulatory action likely; potential officer/director personal liability |

### Likelihood Scale

| Level | Label          | Description                                                        |
| ----- | -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Remote         | No known precedent; would require exceptional circumstances        |
| 2     | Unlikely       | Limited precedent; requires specific triggering events             |
| 3     | Possible       | Some precedent; triggering events are foreseeable                  |
| 4     | Likely         | Clear precedent; triggering events common in similar situations    |
| 5     | Almost Certain | Strong precedent or pattern; triggering events present or imminent |

## Risk Level Actions

| Level              | Escalation                       | Review Cadence          | Key Actions                                                                                                                                                      |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------- | ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **GREEN** (1-4)    | None required                    | Quarterly/annually      | Accept, document in register, monitor                                                                                                                            |
| **YELLOW** (5-9)   | Assign owner; brief stakeholders | Monthly or on trigger   | Mitigate with specific controls; define escalation triggers                                                                                                      |
| **ORANGE** (10-15) | Senior counsel; brief leadership | Weekly or at milestones | Develop mitigation plan; consider outside counsel; full risk memo; define contingency                                                                            |
| **RED** (16-25)    | GC, C-suite, and/or Board        | Daily or more frequent  | Engage outside counsel immediately; establish response team; consider insurance notification; activate crisis management if reputational risk; preserve evidence |

## Risk Categories

Contract, Regulatory, Litigation, IP, Data Privacy, Employment, Corporate, Other

## When to Engage Outside Counsel

### Mandatory

- Active litigation filed against or by the organization
- Government investigation or law enforcement inquiry
- Potential criminal liability for org or personnel
- Securities disclosure or filing implications
- Board-level matters requiring notification or approval

### Strongly Recommended

- Novel legal issues or unsettled law
- Jurisdictional complexity or conflicting requirements across jurisdictions
- Material financial exposure exceeding risk tolerance thresholds
- Specialized expertise not available in-house (antitrust, FCPA, patent prosecution)
- New regulations requiring compliance program development
- M&A due diligence, structuring, or regulatory approvals

### Consider

- Complex contract disputes with material counterparties
- Employment claims (discrimination, harassment, wrongful termination, whistleblower)
- Potential data breaches triggering notification obligations
- IP infringement allegations involving material products
- Insurance coverage disputes for material claims

## Gotchas

- **Severity and likelihood are not independent** -- a high-severity risk often increases its own likelihood because adversaries (plaintiffs, regulators) are more motivated to pursue larger targets; do not assess the two dimensions in isolation.
- **Risk scores compress nuance into false precision** -- a 3x3 and a 9x1 both score 9 but require completely different responses; always present the individual severity and likelihood ratings alongside the composite score.
- **"Accepted" risks still need monitoring** -- risks classified as GREEN and accepted are frequently forgotten; they should remain in the register with a review cadence because conditions change.
- **Residual risk after mitigation is often underestimated** -- teams tend to assume mitigations will work perfectly; always reassess the risk score after mitigations are in place rather than assuming it dropped to the target level.
- **Regulatory risk likelihood is discontinuous** -- it stays low until an enforcement wave hits the sector, then jumps to near-certain overnight; monitor enforcement trends as leading indicators rather than relying on historical frequency.
