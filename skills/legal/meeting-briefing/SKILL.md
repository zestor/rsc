# Meeting Briefing

## Meeting-Type Prep Requirements

| Meeting Type                | Key Prep Needs                                                                                                         |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Deal Review**             | Contract status, open issues, counterparty history, negotiation strategy, approval requirements, comparable deal terms |
| **Board / Committee**       | Legal department update, risk register highlights, regulatory developments, pending resolutions, litigation summary    |
| **Vendor Call**             | Agreement status, open issues, performance metrics, relationship history, negotiation objectives                       |
| **Regulatory / Government** | Matter background, compliance posture, prior communications, outside counsel coordination, privilege considerations    |
| **Litigation / Dispute**    | Case status, recent developments, strategy, settlement parameters, evidence preservation status                        |
| **Cross-Functional**        | Legal implications of business decisions, risk assessment, compliance requirements                                     |

## Briefing Structure

A meeting brief should include these sections as relevant:

1. **Meeting details**: Time, duration, location, attendees with roles and interests
2. **Background and context**: Why this meeting is happening, relevant history
3. **Key documents**: What to review beforehand and where to find it
4. **Open issues**: Status, owner, priority for each
5. **Legal considerations**: Risks, regulatory implications, privilege concerns
6. **Talking points**: Key positions to advance with supporting rationale
7. **Questions to raise**: Open items that need answers from other attendees
8. **Decisions needed**: Options with recommendations
9. **Red lines / non-negotiables**: Positions that cannot be conceded (for negotiation meetings)
10. **Prior meeting follow-up**: Outstanding action items from previous meetings with these participants
11. **Preparation gaps**: Information that could not be found or verified

## Privilege Considerations by Meeting Type

| Meeting Type                | Privilege Risk | Guidance                                                                                    |
| --------------------------- | -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Regulatory / Government** | High           | Clarify what can and cannot be discussed; waiver risk if legal advice shared with regulator |
| **Board / Committee**       | Medium         | Board communications may be discoverable; separate legal advice from business updates       |
| **Cross-Functional**        | Medium         | Privilege may not extend to purely business discussions even if lawyer is present           |
| **Deal Review**             | Low-Medium     | Negotiation strategy is generally privileged; business terms are not                        |
| **Vendor Call**             | Low            | Privilege unlikely to attach unless seeking legal advice during the call                    |

## Action Item Requirements

Every action item captured from a meeting must have:

- Specific, actionable description (not "follow up on contract")
- Single named owner (not a team)
- Concrete deadline date (not "soon" or "ASAP")
- Dependencies noted if the item is blocked on another action or external input
- Type classification: legal team action, business team action, external action (counterparty/outside counsel), or follow-up meeting to schedule

## Gotchas

- **Privilege is waived by overcirculation** -- sharing legal advice in a meeting with non-essential business attendees can waive attorney-client privilege over that advice; assess the attendee list for privilege risk before the meeting.
- **Board meeting minutes are discoverable** -- unlike attorney-client communications, board minutes are generally not privileged; sensitive legal analysis should be delivered in a separate privileged memo, not embedded in the minutes.
- **"No agenda" meetings still need legal prep** -- informal meetings with counterparties or regulators can generate binding oral commitments or admissions; prepare talking points and red lines even when there is no formal agenda.
- **Action items without deadlines never get done** -- legal action items from meetings have a near-zero completion rate when assigned without specific dates; always pin a date even if it is arbitrary.
- **Prior meeting context decays fast** -- if more than two weeks have passed since the last meeting with the same participants, re-pull recent correspondence; stale context leads to repeating resolved issues or missing new developments.
