# Customer Research

Conduct multi-source research to answer customer questions. Prioritize authoritative sources, synthesize across inputs, and clearly communicate confidence levels.

## Source Priority (search in this order)

| Tier                   | Sources                                                         | Confidence              |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| **1 — Official**       | Product docs, KB/wiki, policy docs, roadmap                     | High                    |
| **2 — Organizational** | CRM records, support tickets, internal docs, meeting notes      | Medium-High             |
| **3 — Team Comms**     | Chat history, email threads, calendar notes                     | Medium                  |
| **4 — External**       | Web search, community forums, third-party docs, analyst reports | Low-Medium              |
| **5 — Inferred**       | Similar situations, analogous customers, general best practices | Low (flag as inference) |

Don't stop at the first result — cross-reference across sources.

## Confidence Levels

- **High**: Confirmed by official docs or multiple corroborating sources. "I'm confident based on [source]."
- **Medium**: Found in informal sources (chat, email) without official corroboration. "This appears to be the case, but I'd confirm with [team]."
- **Low**: Inferred from related info, outdated sources, or contradictory findings. "My best assessment is [answer], but this should be verified."
- **Unable to Determine**: No relevant info found. "I recommend reaching out to [team] for a definitive answer."

## Synthesis Format

```
Direct Answer: [Bottom-line — lead with this]
Confidence: [High / Medium / Low]

Supporting Evidence:
- [Source 1]: [What it says]
- [Source 2]: [Corroborates or adds nuance]

Caveats:
- [Limitations or conditions]

Recommendation:
- [Ready to share with customer? Verification needed?]
```

## When to Escalate vs. Answer Directly

**Answer directly when:** official docs clearly address it, multiple sources corroborate, it's factual and non-sensitive, doesn't involve commitments/timelines/pricing.

**Escalate when:** involves roadmap commitments, pricing/legal/contract terms, security/compliance/data handling, could set precedent, contradictory sources found, or wrong answer could make things worse.

## Gotchas

- **Never present uncertain information as fact to a customer** — confidence levels exist for a reason. A wrong answer with confidence destroys more trust than "I'm checking on that."
- **Chat messages are context, not authority** — someone's offhand Slack message is not the same as documentation. Cross-reference before citing.
- **Outdated docs are worse than no docs** — always check dates. A 2-year-old runbook for a feature that's been redesigned twice will lead you astray.
- **"I've answered similar questions before" is not the same as "I know the answer"** — product changes, policy changes. Verify against current sources even for familiar topics.
- **Don't skip Tier 1 sources** — jumping straight to chat or web because it's faster often yields incomplete or incorrect answers.
