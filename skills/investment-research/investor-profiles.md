# Investor Profiles

Reference profiles for **Imitate** mode. Each profile defines quantitative screening criteria and qualitative assessment factors.

## Warren Buffett — Economic Moats & Owner Earnings

**Philosophy:** Buy wonderful companies at fair prices. Hold forever. Margin of safety.

**Quantitative Criteria:**

- ROE > 15% consistently (5-year average)
- Debt/Equity < 0.5
- Free cash flow positive and growing over 5 years
- Net margin > 15%
- P/E reasonable relative to growth (< 20 preferred, < 25 acceptable for quality)
- Consistent revenue growth (no erratic swings)

**Qualitative Factors:**

- Durable competitive advantage (brand, switching costs, network effects, cost advantages)
- Simple, understandable business model
- Capable and honest management (insider ownership, capital allocation track record)
- Pricing power — can raise prices without losing customers
- Low capital intensity — generates earnings without heavy reinvestment

## Peter Lynch — Growth at a Reasonable Price

**Philosophy:** Buy what you know. Find underfollowed growth stories before Wall Street catches on.

**Quantitative Criteria:**

- PEG ratio < 1 (P/E divided by EPS growth rate)
- Revenue growth > 15% annually
- P/E below the earnings growth rate
- Positive earnings (no money-losing speculations)
- Manageable debt (debt/equity < 0.8)
- Strong inventory-to-sales trends (inventory growing slower than sales)

**Qualitative Factors:**

- Underfollowed by analysts (low institutional ownership, sparse coverage)
- Strong niche position or dominant in a boring industry
- "Buy what you know" — products/services the average person can understand
- Insider buying activity
- Company is buying back shares

## Stanley Druckenmiller — Macro + Momentum + Conviction

**Philosophy:** Concentrate on your best ideas. Ride macro trends with size. Cut losses fast.

**Quantitative Criteria:**

- Relative strength vs sector and market (outperforming over 3–6 months)
- Positive earnings revision trends (consensus estimates rising)
- Sector momentum (sector ETF outperforming SPY)
- Revenue acceleration (sequential quarter-over-quarter growth increasing)
- RSI between 50–70 (strong but not overextended)

**Qualitative Factors:**

- Clear macro tailwind (secular trend, policy shift, structural demand)
- Asymmetric risk/reward setup (limited downside, large upside potential)
- Management executing well (beating guidance, expanding margins)
- Position in the economic cycle favors the sector
- Concentrated bet — conviction should be high enough to warrant a large position

## George Soros — Reflexivity & Macro Imbalances

**Philosophy:** Markets create their own reality through feedback loops. Find the reflexive process before it reverses.

**Quantitative Criteria:**

- Market valuation at extremes (CAPE ratio vs historical range)
- Credit spread widening/tightening trends
- Currency stress indicators (rapid moves in FX pairs)
- Divergence between asset prices and fundamentals
- Volume and momentum confirming the narrative

**Qualitative Factors:**

- Self-reinforcing feedback loops (rising prices → more buying → rising prices)
- Narrative shifts in the market (consensus changing direction)
- Policy changes that create or destroy reflexive dynamics
- Institutional positioning extremes (crowded trades vulnerable to reversal)
- Gap between perception and reality in a sector or macro theme

## Ray Dalio — Risk Parity & Macro Cycles

**Philosophy:** Diversify across uncorrelated return streams. Understand where you are in the long-term debt cycle.

**Quantitative Criteria:**

- Asset class correlations (build portfolios where correlations are low or negative)
- Real yields (nominal yields minus inflation expectations)
- Debt cycle positioning (total debt/GDP, credit growth rate, interest coverage)
- Risk-adjusted returns (Sharpe ratio, max drawdown)
- Balance between growth-sensitive and inflation-sensitive assets

**Qualitative Factors:**

- Long-term debt cycle phase (early expansion, bubble, deleveraging, reflation)
- Paradigm shift signals (structural changes in monetary/fiscal policy)
- Central bank policy trajectory and constraints
- Geopolitical risk factors affecting global capital flows
- Historical analogues to current macro environment

## Bill Ackman — Activist Value & High-Quality Franchises

**Philosophy:** Find great businesses that are temporarily misunderstood. Push for operational improvements if needed.

**Quantitative Criteria:**

- Free cash flow yield > 7%
- ROIC > 15%
- Moderate leverage (debt/EBITDA < 3x)
- Gross margin > 50% (franchise-quality business)
- Trading below intrinsic value by 30%+ (margin of safety)
- Stable or growing revenue base

**Qualitative Factors:**

- Operational improvement potential (cost cuts, margin expansion, capital allocation)
- Misunderstood or out-of-favor business (temporary overhang vs structural problem)
- Clear catalyst for value realization (management change, spin-off, strategic review)
- High barriers to entry protecting the franchise
- Durable demand — product/service is essential to customers

## Michael Burry — Deep Value & Contrarian

**Philosophy:** Buy what nobody wants. Find assets trading below liquidation value. Be early and be right.

**Quantitative Criteria:**

- Price/Book < 1 (trading below book value)
- P/E < 10 (deep value territory)
- Asset-heavy balance sheet below liquidation value
- Enterprise Value / EBIT < 8
- Net current asset value (NCAV) > market cap (Ben Graham net-net)
- Positive free cash flow despite depressed earnings

**Qualitative Factors:**

- Out-of-favor sector (cyclical trough, regulatory overhang, sentiment collapse)
- Ignored small caps with no analyst coverage
- Distressed situations with identifiable catalysts (restructuring, asset sales, management change)
- Market is pricing in permanent impairment where recovery is likely
- Insider buying during the downturn

## Cathie Wood — Disruptive Innovation

**Philosophy:** Invest in technologies on exponential growth curves. Wright's Law cost declines create winner-take-most dynamics.

**Quantitative Criteria:**

- Total addressable market (TAM) expanding rapidly (>20% CAGR)
- Revenue growth > 25% annually
- R&D intensity > 15% of revenue (investing heavily in innovation)
- Gross margin expanding or stable at high levels (>50%)
- Revenue per employee increasing (operating leverage)
- Market share gains vs incumbents

**Qualitative Factors:**

- Platform technology enabling multiple applications
- Wright's Law cost curves (unit costs declining predictably with cumulative production)
- Regulatory tailwinds or neutral regulatory environment
- Convergence of multiple technologies amplifying growth (AI + robotics + energy storage)
- First-mover or strong second-mover advantage in a nascent market
- Management with a clear long-term vision and willingness to sacrifice near-term profits
